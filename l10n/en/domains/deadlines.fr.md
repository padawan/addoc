Different dates exist:

- Renewal: _normal_ process to add one or more years to the current domain expiration date.
- Late renewal: optional period proposed by the registry allowing you to renew the domain after its expiration date.
- Erase at alwaysdata: date when the domain is no longer managed at alwaysdata but can optionally be restored to the registry. Contact our [support](https://admin.alwaysdata.com/support/add/).
- Restoration possible: the domain is _redemption_ ; it can be restored to the registry, but its billing is _more_ by the registry.
- Registry Destruction: date from which the domain can _become available again_ at registration. Some registers add a random period at that date before the domain is destroyed to allow the first arrival, first served.
